=== LOAN GUIDELINES AND POLICIES ===

--- HOME LOAN POLICIES ---
Eligibility Criteria:
- Minimum age: 21 years (salaried), 25 years (self-employed)
- Maximum age at loan maturity: 60-70 years (salaried), 65-75 years (self-employed)
- Minimum income: Varies by bank and loan amount
- Minimum CIBIL score: 700-750 recommended
- Employment stability: Minimum 2 years in current job (salaried)
- Business continuity: Minimum 2-3 years (self-employed)

Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio (RBI Guidelines):
- Loans up to INR 30 lakhs: Maximum LTV 90%
- Loans between INR 30-75 lakhs: Maximum LTV 80%
- Loans above INR 75 lakhs: Maximum LTV 75%
- Remaining amount must be paid as down payment by borrower

Interest Rates:
- Floating rate: 8.5% to 10.5% per annum (linked to repo rate/MCLR)
- Fixed rate: Slightly higher than floating, remains constant
- Women borrowers concession: 0.05% to 0.10% on interest rate
- Rates subject to change based on RBI monetary policy

Home Loan Tax Benefits:
- Principal repayment: Up to INR 1.5 lakhs under Section 80C
- Interest payment: Up to INR 2 lakhs under Section 24(b) for self-occupied
- Let-out property: No limit on interest deduction
- First-time buyers: Additional INR 50,000 under Section 80EEA (conditions apply)
- Stamp duty and registration: Qualifies under Section 80C in year of payment

Documentation Required:
- Identity proof: Aadhaar, PAN, Passport
- Address proof: Aadhaar, utility bill, rent agreement
- Income proof (salaried): Last 3 months salary slips, Form 16, ITR for 2 years
- Income proof (self-employed): ITR for 3 years, audited P&L, balance sheet
- Bank statements: Last 6 months
- Property documents: Sale agreement, NOC from builder, title deed
- Photographs: Passport size

Processing Time:
- Document submission to approval: 7-15 working days
- Subject to property verification and legal checks
- Pre-approved offers for existing customers: Faster processing

Charges:
- Processing fee: 0.25% to 1% of loan amount
- GST: 18% on processing fee
- Legal and technical charges: INR 5,000 to INR 15,000
- Stamp duty on mortgage deed: As per state government rates
- Franking charges: 0.1% to 0.2% of loan agreement value

Prepayment and Foreclosure:
- Floating rate loans (individual borrowers): No prepayment penalty (RBI guideline)
- Fixed rate loans: 2-4% of outstanding principal
- Part prepayment: Option to reduce EMI or tenure
- Lock-in period: Some banks have 6-12 month lock-in

Balance Transfer:
- Transfer outstanding loan to another bank for lower interest rate
- New bank processing fee applicable
- Foreclosure charges on old loan (if fixed rate)
- Top-up loan option available during transfer

Under-Construction Property:
- Pre-EMI: Pay only interest on disbursed amount during construction
- Full EMI: Start full EMI from first disbursement
- Disbursement in tranches based on construction progress
- Tax benefit on interest: Available only after possession

PMAY (Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana):
- EWS/LIG (income up to INR 6 lakhs): 6.5% subsidy on loans up to INR 6 lakhs
- MIG-I (income up to INR 12 lakhs): 4% subsidy on loans up to INR 9 lakhs
- MIG-II (income up to INR 18 lakhs): 3% subsidy on loans up to INR 12 lakhs
- For first-time home buyers only
- Subject to scheme availability and government updates

--- PERSONAL LOAN POLICIES ---
Eligibility:
- Minimum age: 21 years
- Maximum age: 60 years (at loan maturity)
- Minimum monthly income (salaried): INR 15,000 per month
- Minimum annual income (self-employed): INR 2,00,000
- Minimum CIBIL score: 700-750
- Employment: Minimum 1-2 years in current organization

Loan Details:
- Loan amount: INR 50,000 to INR 40 lakhs
- Tenure: 12 to 60 months (some banks up to 84 months)
- Interest rates: 10% to 18% per annum
- Processing fee: 0.5% to 2.5% of loan amount
- GST on processing fee: 18%
- Disbursement: Within 1-3 working days for complete applications

Prepayment:
- Floating rate: No prepayment penalty (RBI guideline)
- Fixed rate: 2-5% of outstanding principal
- Lock-in period: 6-12 months typically
- Part prepayment: Reduces principal, saves interest

--- VEHICLE LOAN POLICIES ---
Eligibility:
- Salaried individuals, self-employed, and businesses
- Minimum age: 21 years
- Maximum age: 65 years (at loan maturity)
- Minimum income: Varies by bank and loan amount

Loan Details:
- Loan amount: Up to 80-90% of on-road price
- Tenure: Up to 7 years for cars, up to 5 years for two-wheelers
- Interest rates: 7% to 12% per annum
- Vehicle serves as collateral (hypothecated to bank)
- Processing fee: INR 500 to INR 5,000

Loan Closure:
- Obtain NOC from bank after full repayment
- Remove hypothecation from RC book at RTO
- Keep closure letter and NOC safely

--- EDUCATION LOAN POLICIES ---
Eligibility:
- Student must have secured admission to recognized institution
- Co-applicant required (parent, guardian, or spouse)
- Indian nationality

Loan Details:
- Loans up to INR 4 lakhs: No collateral required
- Loans from INR 4-7.5 lakhs: Third-party guarantee required
- Loans above INR 7.5 lakhs: Collateral required
- Coverage: Tuition fees, hostel, books, equipment, travel

Repayment:
- Moratorium period: Course duration plus 6-12 months
- Repayment tenure: Up to 15 years
- Interest subsidy available for economically weaker sections

Tax Benefit:
- Section 80E: Deduction on entire interest paid
- No limit on deduction amount
- Available for 8 consecutive years from start of repayment
- For self, spouse, children, or legal ward

--- GOLD LOAN POLICIES ---
Eligibility:
- Any individual above 18 years
- Must own gold jewellery or coins
- No income proof required
- No CIBIL score requirement

Loan Details:
- Loan amount: Up to 75% of gold value (as per RBI guideline)
- Interest rates: 7% to 12% per annum
- Tenure: Up to 12-24 months
- Quick disbursement: Within hours
- Minimal documentation

Processing:
- Gold assessed by bank's appraiser
- Gold stored in bank vault during loan tenure
- Return gold upon full repayment
- Auction of gold if default occurs after notice

--- LOAN AGAINST PROPERTY (LAP) ---
Eligibility:
- Owner of residential or commercial property
- Age: 21 to 65 years
- Regular income source

Loan Details:
- Loan amount: Up to 60-70% of property market value
- Interest rates: 9% to 12% per annum
- Tenure: Up to 15-20 years
- Processing fee: 1-2% of loan amount
- End use: Any legitimate business or personal purpose

--- BUSINESS LOAN / MSME LOAN POLICIES ---
Eligibility:
- Business vintage: Minimum 2-3 years
- Annual turnover: As per bank policy
- GST registration: Required for most business loans
- CIBIL score: 700+ recommended

Government Schemes:
- MUDRA Loan: Up to INR 10 lakhs for micro enterprises
  - Shishu: Up to INR 50,000
  - Kishor: INR 50,000 to INR 5 lakhs
  - Tarun: INR 5 lakhs to INR 10 lakhs
- CGTMSE: Credit guarantee for MSMEs without collateral
- Stand-Up India: Loans for SC/ST and women entrepreneurs

--- LOAN RECOVERY POLICIES (RBI GUIDELINES) ---
Recovery Agent Conduct:
- Contact hours: 8 AM to 7 PM only
- No abusive, threatening, or intimidating behavior
- Cannot contact family members (unless guarantors)
- Must carry valid identification
- No physical force or harassment allowed
- Violations to be reported to bank grievance cell or RBI Ombudsman

NPA Classification:
- Loan classified as NPA if EMI overdue for more than 90 days
- Sub-standard: NPA for up to 12 months
- Doubtful: NPA for more than 12 months
- Loss assets: No recovery expected

SARFAESI Act:
- Applicable to secured loans above INR 1 lakh
- Bank issues 60-day notice before asset seizure
- Asset can be seized without court order after notice period
- Proceeds from asset sale used to recover dues
- Borrower can approach Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT)

Loan Restructuring:
- Available for genuine financial hardship cases
- Options: Extended tenure, reduced EMI, interest waiver
- Requires bank approval and assessment
- Impact on credit score may vary

--- EMI CALCULATION AND MANAGEMENT ---
EMI Formula:
EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / [(1+r)^n - 1]
Where:
- P = Principal loan amount
- r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100)
- n = Number of monthly installments

Example Calculations:
- INR 5,00,000 at 10% for 5 years: EMI = INR 10,624
- INR 10,00,000 at 8.5% for 10 years: EMI = INR 12,400
- INR 30,00,000 at 9% for 20 years: EMI = INR 26,992

Amortization:
- Early EMIs: Higher interest component, lower principal
- Later EMIs: Lower interest component, higher principal
- Total interest paid decreases with shorter tenure
- Part prepayment reduces total interest significantly

EMI Bounce:
- Penalty: INR 300 to INR 750 per bounce
- Late payment charge: 1-2% per month on overdue amount
- Negative CIBIL score impact
- Repeated bounces may trigger loan recall

NACH/ECS for EMI:
- Automatic debit on due date
- Register via branch or Aadhaar e-sign
- Ensure sufficient balance before due date
- Cancellation requires 3-7 working days notice

--- CIBIL AND CREDIT SCORE POLICIES ---
Score Ranges:
- 750-900: Excellent - Best rates and easy approval
- 700-749: Good - Approval likely with competitive rates
- 650-699: Fair - Approval possible with higher rates
- 600-649: Poor - Difficult approval, very high rates
- 300-599: Very Poor - Likely rejection

Factors Affecting Score:
- Payment history: 35% weightage
- Credit utilization: 30% weightage
- Length of credit history: 15% weightage
- Credit mix: 10% weightage
- New credit inquiries: 10% weightage

Improving Credit Score:
- Pay all EMIs and credit card bills on time
- Keep credit utilization below 30%
- Avoid multiple loan applications simultaneously
- Maintain mix of secured and unsecured credit
- Check report regularly for errors
- Score improvement timeline: 6-12 months

Dispute Resolution:
- File dispute at cibil.com
- CIBIL forwards to bank for verification
- Resolution in 30-45 days
- Escalate to RBI Ombudsman if unresolved

--- PRIORITY SECTOR LENDING (PSL) ---
RBI Mandate for Banks:
- Total PSL target: 40% of Adjusted Net Bank Credit (ANBC)
- Agriculture: 18% of ANBC
- Micro enterprises: 7.5% of ANBC
- Weaker sections: 12% of ANBC
- Shortfall contribution: To RIDF or other RBI funds

Kisan Credit Card (KCC):
- For farmers to meet agricultural input needs
- Interest rate: 7% per annum (with interest subvention)
- Flexible repayment aligned with crop cycles
- Reviewed annually based on cropping pattern
- Issued under RBI/NABARD guidelines

--- INTEREST RATE FRAMEWORK ---
Benchmark Rates:
- Repo Rate: Set by RBI, affects all lending rates
- MCLR: Internal bank benchmark, resets periodically (monthly to annually)
- EBLR (External Benchmark Lending Rate): Directly linked to repo rate
- Resets quarterly for EBLR-linked loans

Rate Types:
- Fixed Rate: Constant throughout tenure or initial period
- Floating Rate: Changes with benchmark rate
- Teaser Rate: Low initial rate that resets to higher rate later (RBI has expressed concerns)

RBI Guidelines on Rates:
- Banks cannot lend below MCLR (with exceptions)
- Floating rate loans for individuals: No prepayment penalty
- Rate change communication: 3 months advance notice required
- Interest rate transparency: Full disclosure mandatory

--- JOINT LOAN POLICIES ---
Co-applicant Benefits:
- Combined income increases loan eligibility
- Both can claim individual tax benefits
- Women co-borrowers may get interest rate concession
- Property must be co-owned in home loans

Co-applicant vs Guarantor:
- Co-applicant: Primary liability, benefits from loan
- Guarantor: Secondary liability (triggered on default), no benefit
- Both face credit score impact on default
- Both must understand full obligations before agreeing
